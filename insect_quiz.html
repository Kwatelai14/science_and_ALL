Insect True or False Quiz

Housefly · Honeybee · Butterfly · Grain Weevil — 30 Questions

🪲 Housefly (Q. 1–8)
1.The housefly (Musca domestica) belongs to the order Diptera, meaning it has only two functional wings.
2.Houseflies can only taste food using their mouthparts.
3.The complete life cycle of a housefly from egg to adult can be completed in as little as 7–10 days under warm conditions.
4.Houseflies are capable of biting humans to feed on blood.
5.A female housefly can lay up to 500 eggs in her lifetime, in batches on decaying organic matter.
6.Houseflies have compound eyes that give them nearly 360° vision.
7.Houseflies can transmit diseases such as cholera, typhoid, and dysentery by contaminating food.
8.The larval stage of the housefly is commonly called a caterpillar.
🐝 Honeybee (Q. 9–16)
9.Honeybees (Apis mellifera) are social insects that live in colonies of up to 60,000 individuals.
10.A queen honeybee mates only once during her lifetime.
11.Worker honeybees are all sterile females.
12.Honeybees communicate the location of food sources to their hive-mates through a "waggle dance."
13.A worker bee dies after using her stinger because the barbed stinger is torn from her abdomen.
14.Drones (male honeybees) are responsible for defending the hive.
15.Beeswax is secreted from glands on the abdomen of worker bees.
16.Honeybees are important pollinators that contribute to the reproduction of about one-third of the world's food crops.
🦋 Butterfly (Q. 17–23)
17.Butterflies belong to the order Lepidoptera, which they share with moths.
18.The process by which a caterpillar transforms into a butterfly inside a chrysalis is called metamorphosis.
19.Butterflies taste with their feet, using taste receptors called chemoreceptors on their tarsi.
20.Most butterflies are nocturnal, meaning they are most active at night.
21.The Monarch butterfly is famous for its long-distance annual migration of up to 4,000 km.
22.Butterfly wings are covered with tiny overlapping scales made of chitin.
23.Adult butterflies feed primarily on solid plant matter using chewing mouthparts.

Detailed Explanations

1
Housefly belongs to Diptera with two wings?
TRUEDiptera means "two wings." Houseflies have one pair of functional wings and a second pair reduced to club-like halteres used for balance.
2
Houseflies taste only with their mouthparts?
FALSEHouseflies also taste with taste receptors (chemoreceptors) located on their feet (tarsi), allowing them to detect food simply by landing on it.
3
Housefly life cycle completes in 7–10 days in warm conditions?
TRUEUnder warm temperatures (~30°C), the housefly can complete its entire egg → larva → pupa → adult cycle in about 7–10 days.
4
Houseflies bite humans for blood?
FALSEThe common housefly has sponging mouthparts and cannot bite. It feeds on liquids by dissolving food with saliva. (The stable fly, a different species, does bite.)
5
Female housefly can lay up to 500 eggs?
TRUEA female housefly lays 5–6 batches of approximately 75–100 eggs each, totalling up to 500 eggs, deposited in decaying organic matter.
6
Houseflies have compound eyes with ~360° vision?
TRUELarge compound eyes on either side of the head give houseflies a very wide field of view, almost 360°, making them hard to sneak up on.
7
Houseflies transmit cholera, typhoid, dysentery?
TRUEHouseflies pick up pathogens on their bodies and in their gut from faeces and waste, then transfer them to human food, spreading many diseases.
8
Housefly larva called a caterpillar?
FALSEHousefly larvae are called maggots, not caterpillars. Caterpillar is the term for the larval stage of butterflies and moths (Lepidoptera).
9
Honeybee colonies up to 60,000 individuals?
TRUEA strong honeybee colony in peak summer may contain 50,000–80,000 bees, including one queen, thousands of drones, and the majority as worker bees.
10
Queen honeybee mates only once?
FALSEA queen mates with multiple drones (10–20) during her nuptial flights, storing enough sperm to fertilise eggs throughout her life of several years.
11
Worker honeybees are sterile females?
TRUEWorkers are underdeveloped females. While they possess ovaries, these are normally suppressed by queen pheromones and they do not mate.
12
Honeybees communicate via a waggle dance?
TRUEDiscovered by Karl von Frisch, the waggle dance encodes direction (relative to the sun) and distance to a food source through movement patterns on the comb.
13
Worker bee dies after stinging due to barbed stinger?
TRUEWhen a worker bee stings a mammal's elastic skin, the barbed stinger lodges in and is torn out along with venom sac and abdominal tissue, killing the bee.
14
Drones defend the hive?
FALSEDrones have no stinger and play no defensive role. Their sole function is to mate with a queen. Defence of the hive is carried out by worker bees.
15
Beeswax is secreted from glands on workers' abdomen?
TRUEWorker bees have four pairs of wax glands on the underside of their abdomen that secrete liquid wax, which solidifies into scales used to build honeycomb.
16
Honeybees pollinate about one-third of food crops?
TRUEBees (including honeybees) are essential pollinators for roughly one-third of global food production, including many fruits, nuts, and vegetables.
17
Butterflies belong to Lepidoptera, shared with moths?
TRUEBoth butterflies and moths belong to Lepidoptera ("scaly-winged"). Butterflies are generally distinguished by clubbed antennae and daytime activity.
18
Caterpillar-to-butterfly transformation is metamorphosis?
TRUEButterflies undergo complete (holometabolous) metamorphosis: egg → larva (caterpillar) → pupa (chrysalis) → adult (imago).
19
Butterflies taste with their feet?
TRUEButterflies have chemoreceptors on their tarsi (feet), so when they land on a plant they can immediately detect whether it is edible or a suitable host for eggs.
20
Most butterflies are nocturnal?
FALSEButterflies are diurnal (active during the day). Moths are typically the nocturnal members of Lepidoptera, though exceptions exist in both groups.
21
Monarch butterfly migrates up to 4,000 km?
TRUEMonarch butterflies undertake one of the longest insect migrations, travelling up to 4,500 km from Canada and the northern US to overwintering sites in Mexico.
22
Butterfly wings covered in tiny chitin scales?
TRUEWing scales (made of chitin) overlap like roof tiles. Their microscopic structure creates iridescent colours through light interference as well as pigment-based colour.
23
Adult butterflies feed on solid matter with chewing mouthparts?
FALSEAdult butterflies have a long, coiled proboscis (not chewing jaws) used to drink nectar and other liquids. Only caterpillars chew solid plant material.
24
Grain weevil is a major stored-grain pest?
TRUESitophilus granarius is one of the most destructive pests of stored grain (wheat, rye, oats, barley) worldwide, causing enormous post-harvest losses.
25
Grain weevils are beetles (Coleoptera)?
TRUEWeevils belong to the family Curculionidae within Coleoptera (beetles), the largest order of insects. They are characterised by their distinctive elongated snout.
26
Female grain weevils lay eggs inside grain kernels?
TRUEThe female bores a hole in a kernel using her rostrum, deposits a single egg, then seals the hole with a waxy plug. The larva develops entirely within the kernel.
27
Adult grain weevils can fly?
FALSEUnlike the closely related rice weevil (S. oryzae), the grain weevil (S. granarius) has vestigial wings and cannot fly. It spreads only by crawling or in infested grain.
28
Grain weevil uses its snout (rostrum) to bore into grain?
TRUEThe rostrum is a distinctive elongated snout bearing mouthparts at its tip, used to excavate feeding cavities and egg-laying holes in hard grain kernels.
29
Grain weevils thrive in warm, humid conditions above 15°C?
TRUEGrain weevils are most active and reproduce fastest at 25–30°C with moderate humidity. Temperatures below 15°C significantly slow development and activity.
30
Grain weevils controlled by cold storage or airtight containers?
TRUERefrigerated storage kills or inhibits weevils, and hermetic (airtight) storage depletes oxygen, causing suffocation. Both are widely used integrated pest management strategies.
